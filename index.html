About Me

I am a computational biologist specializing in Genetics and Epigenetics analysis of adult and pediatric brain tumors through multi-omics, spatial transcriptomics, and machine learning. My research aims to uncover the molecular and cellular mechanisms driving tumor heterogeneity by leveraging cutting-edge AI and data-driven approaches. I am particularly focused on developing interpretable and clinically relevant computational frameworks to support personalized cancer therapy and advance the field of precision oncology.

Research Summary

Cancer progression is driven not only by genetic alterations within tumor cells but also by dynamic interactions in the tumor microenvironment. My research aims to decode these interactions through the lens of transcriptional, epigenetic, and imaging-based data. I focus on integrating single-cell and spatial transcriptomics, DNA methylation, and histopathological imaging using deep learning frameworks to identify clinically relevant tumor and immune cell states. This approach enhances our understanding of tumor heterogeneity, therapy resistance, and patient outcomes in medulloblastomas and gliomas.

My research is organized into four key themes:

  • Integrative multi-omics analysis of central nervous system (CNS) tumors to uncover molecular drivers of progression
  • AI-based prediction of malignant and immune cell states from H&E-stained histopathology images
  • Spatial mapping of tumor-immune interactions using transcriptomics and image data
  • Development of interpretable machine learning models (ViT, GNN, MLP) for clinical biomarker discovery

Biography

Dr. Saleembhasha Asanigari is a Senior Bioinformatics Research Scientist at the National Cancer Institute (NCI), NIH. He holds a Ph.D. in Biochemistry from the University of Hyderabad and has developed expertise in cancer systems biology, single-cell omics, spatial transcriptomics, and machine learning-based approaches for tumor classification and biomarker discovery.

After earning his doctorate, Dr. Asanigari has led integrative research efforts that bridge histopathology, epigenetics, and gene expression, focusing particularly on pediatric brain tumors such as medulloblastomas. His recent work includes applying Vision Transformers (ViT), Graph Neural Networks (GNN), and interpretable MLPs to predict molecular cell states from H&E-stained diagnostic slides, significantly enhancing our understanding of tumor heterogeneity and prognosis.

He has actively contributed to several collaborative research projects across NIH divisions, mentors junior scientists, and serves as a peer reviewer for international journals. His long-term vision involves establishing AI-guided frameworks for cancer precision medicine in India by leveraging population-specific genomic and imaging data to promote equitable healthcare innovation.
