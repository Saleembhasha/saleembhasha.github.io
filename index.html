About Me

I am a computational biologist focused on unraveling the complexities of adult and pediatric brain tumors using integrative multi-omics, spatial biology, and machine learning. My goal is to develop interpretable AI tools that inform personalized treatment strategies and advance cancer precision medicine.

Research Summary

Cancer progression is driven not only by genetic alterations within tumor cells but also by dynamic interactions in the tumor microenvironment. My research aims to decode these interactions through the lens of transcriptional, epigenetic, and imaging-based data. I focus on integrating single-cell and spatial transcriptomics, DNA methylation, and histopathological imaging using deep learning frameworks to identify clinically relevant tumor and immune cell states. This approach enhances our understanding of tumor heterogeneity, therapy resistance, and patient outcomes in medulloblastomas and gliomas.

My research is organized into four key themes:

  • Integrative multi-omics analysis of central nervous system (CNS) tumors to uncover molecular drivers of progression
  • AI-based prediction of malignant and immune cell states from H&E-stained histopathology images
  • Spatial mapping of tumor-immune interactions using transcriptomics and image data
  • Development of interpretable machine learning models (ViT, GNN, MLP) for clinical biomarker discovery
